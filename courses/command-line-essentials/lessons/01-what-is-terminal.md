# What is the Terminal?

Imagine you have a personal assistant. You could tell them in words, or you could type precise instructions. The Terminal is like typing instructions directly to your computer.

Instead of clicking buttons, you type commands.

## Why Learn This?

Most tutorials online assume you know the Terminal. Learning it opens doors to:
- Installing developer tools
- Running AI coding assistants
- Working with servers
- Automating boring tasks

## How to Open It

**Mac:** Press `Cmd + Space`, type "Terminal", press Enter

**Windows:** Press `Win + X`, select "Terminal" or "Command Prompt"

**Linux:** Press `Ctrl + Alt + T`

## Your First Look

You will see something like this:

```
kasidis-MacBook:~ kasidis$
```

This is called the **prompt**. It tells you:
- Your computer name (`kasidis-MacBook`)
- Where you are (`~` means home folder)
- Your username (`kasidis`)

The blinking cursor `|` is waiting for your command.

## The Key Idea

Think of the Terminal as a conversation with your computer. You ask, it answers. No clicking, no dragging—just text.

> In this course, we will speak "computer" together. By the end, you will feel at home in this text-based world.
