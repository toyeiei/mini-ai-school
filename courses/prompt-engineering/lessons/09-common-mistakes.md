# Common Mistakes

Learn from these frequent pitfalls.

## Mistake 1: Being Too Vague

**Bad:** "Help me with my business"

**Better:** "I'm starting a home bakery. Help me create a pricing strategy for custom cakes."

## Mistake 2: Not Saying What You Don't Want

**Bad:** "Write a cover letter"

**Better:** "Write a cover letter. Do NOT use clichés like 'I am a hard worker' or 'passionate about my work.' Focus on specific achievements with numbers."

## Mistake 3: Conflicting Instructions

**Bad:** "Write something short but comprehensive"

**Short** and **comprehensive** can conflict. Pick one or be clearer.

**Better:** "Write a comprehensive overview in 300 words" or "Write a short summary in 3 sentences"

## Mistake 4: Forgetting the Audience

**Bad:** "Explain blockchain"

**Better:** "Explain blockchain to my mom who uses Facebook but has never heard of cryptocurrency."

## Mistake 5: One-Shot Expectations

Thinking one prompt should give perfect results every time is unrealistic. Plan for iteration.

## Mistake 6: Not Using Available Features

Many AI tools have:
- Temperature/randomness controls
- Context length settings
- System prompts
- Conversation memory

Learn what's available and use it.

## Quick Checklist

Before sending, ask:
- Is my goal clear?
- Did I include relevant context?
- Did I specify the format?
- Did I mention constraints?
- Is there anything to avoid?
