# Be Clear & Specific

The more specific you are, the better the result.

## Instead of "Write something"

Tell it:
- What to write
- For whom
- How long
- What tone

## Example: Email Request

**Vague:**
```
Write an email to my boss.
```

**Specific:**
```
Write a professional email to my manager explaining that the project will be delayed by 3 days due to unexpected technical issues. Keep it under 150 words. Be apologetic but confident that quality won't be compromised.
```

The specific version gives the AI everything it needs.

## Key Specificity Tips

1. **Define the audience:** "For a 10-year-old" vs "For software engineers"
2. **Set the length:** "3 sentences" vs "A detailed report"
3. **Choose the tone:** "Casual and friendly" vs "Formal and professional"
4. **State the purpose:** "To apologize" vs "To convince them to change their mind"

## Practice

Take this vague prompt and make it specific:

```
Write about climate change.
```

**Your version:** ___________________________________

**Example improvement:**
```
Write a 300-word overview of climate change for high school students. Explain the basic science simply. Include one example of how it affects daily life. Tone: informative but not alarming.
```

## When in Doubt, Over-Explain

It's easier for AI to ignore extra details than to guess what you meant. Don't be afraid to be thorough.
